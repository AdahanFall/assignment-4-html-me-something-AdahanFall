A sample list of some of the subreddits that I subscribe to.

This isn't a complete list. I just wanted to pick a few things that I thought were representative of the things I like. Also, I get severe writer's block unless given a very specific topic to talk about (I have literally spent hours looking at a blank screen before when given too much freedom in a writing assignment), and this is what a friend told me to do.

Chess

I'm not going to lie and say that I'm any good at the game, but I love solving chess puzzles and enjoy watching the experts play and seeing analysis of their games. I'm going to be honest. I don't understand a lot of the really deep analysis... Mostly I just struggle with the puzzles that are posted and marvel at how good Magnus Carlsen is, just like everyone else. As a deep cut, sometimes it's fun to take a side trip over to /r/anarchychess.

Green Bay Packers

I may not be a huge fan of the NFL anymore (especially with with they treated the city of St. Louis), but I was born in Wisconsin and I inherited this fandom from my family. I'll always have a soft spot in my heart for this team. I absolutely love their odd origin story, and their status as a community-owned non-profit team that exists despite the insane corporate influence of the NFL.

Overwatch

I don't play this game nearly as much as I used to. Part of it is the fact that it's gotten stale. Part of it is because I don't have much time to game anymore. And yet another part of it is some of the news stories that have come out about working conditions there... But I still keep this sub in my subscriptions, and check out the PotGs that people post every now and then.

St. Louis

Because sometimes I want to see people complaining about the STLPD, the Metrolink, provel cheese, and whatever else is going on in the area. I might not be a "true St-Louisan" because I was born in Wisconsin, but I've been in the area for almost 20 years and I'm glad to be here.

Board Games

Because I'm seriously thinking of thinking one of those huge 6x6 Kallax shelves from Ikea to house my collection of games. Yeah, I already list /r/chess as one of my subs, but this is a bit different. This encompasses the entire field of Euro-style games. The pandemic has made it a bit harder to find groups to play with, but it's still fun to get a group of your close (and vaccinated) friends together around the gaming table.
